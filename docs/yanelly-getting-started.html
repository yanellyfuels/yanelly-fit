Your Website · Getting Started

Welcome to your website 🥑

Hi Yanelly! This is everything you need to take ownership of your site and start making it your own — no tech experience required. Just follow the steps in order. Each one is a few clicks, and Jon is one message away if you ever get stuck.

  1. Create your free GitHub account

    Go to github.com and click Sign up. GitHub is just the place your website's files live online — think of it as the filing cabinet for your site.

    Then send Jon your new username so he can hand the website over to you.

    Tip: write your username and password down somewhere safe — you'll use this account from now on.

  2. Accept your website

    Jon will transfer the website to your account. You'll get an email (and a GitHub notification) asking you to accept it — just click Accept.

    That's it — the website is now officially yours. 🎉

  3. Switch your website on

    Open your project on GitHub, click Settings, then Pages on the left. Under "Source," choose GitHub Actions. Also make sure the project is set to Public.

    Give it a minute or two — your site will be live at:

    https://[your-username].github.io/yanelly-fit/

    Important: your web address includes your username, so it'll be a little different from before. Once it's live, update the link in your Instagram bio to this new address.

  4. Add Jon as your helper

    So Jon can jump in whenever you need a hand: go to Settings → Collaborators → Add people, and type in his username:

    brojonbeast

  5. Connect your AI assistant (ChatGPT Codex)

    This is the magic part — your AI that edits the website for you. In ChatGPT, open Codex, click Connect to GitHub, approve the pop-up, and choose your yanelly-fit project.

    Heads up: Codex comes with the paid ChatGPT plans (Plus, Pro, or Business). Once it's connected, you're ready to build.

  6. Make changes anytime — just ask

    Tell Codex what you want in plain English, like:

    "Add a recipe card for protein pancakes"  or  "Change my headline to ___."

    Codex prepares the change and shows it to you as a "pull request" (a suggested update). When you like it, click Merge — and your live site updates in about a minute.

    You can't break anything. Nothing changes on your real website until you click Merge — so feel free to experiment. You can also edit text right on GitHub using the little pencil icon.
