The fundamentals

Nutrition basics

Start here. About 90% of healthy eating comes down to these few ideas.

🍽️

Build a balanced meal

Picture your plate in simple sections:

  • ½ fruits & veggies — color + fiber
  • ¼ protein — fullness + muscle
  • ¼ carbs — your main energy
  • + a thumb of healthy fats & water
🍗

Protein sources

Builds and repairs muscle, keeps you full longest.

  • Chicken, turkey, lean beef
  • Eggs & egg whites
  • Greek yogurt & cottage cheese
  • Fish, tuna, tofu, beans
🍠

Carb sources

Your main fuel — especially around workouts.

  • Rice, oats, quinoa
  • Potatoes & sweet potatoes
  • Whole-grain bread & pasta
  • Fruit & beans
🥑

Healthy fats

Supports hormones and keeps meals satisfying.

  • Avocado & olive oil
  • Nuts, seeds & nut butter
  • Salmon & fatty fish
  • Cheese & whole eggs
🛒

Grocery staples

Keep these around and a good meal is 10 minutes away.

  • Chicken, eggs, ground turkey
  • Greek yogurt, cottage cheese
  • Rice, oats, tortillas
  • Frozen produce, tuna, PB
💡

Explained simply

  • Calories = energy. Match them to your goal.
  • No food is "bad." It's the overall pattern.
  • Protein + fiber = the fullness power couple.
  • Consistency > perfection. Always.

The 30-second method

One plate. Three sections. Done.

No weighing, no tracking, no math. Fill half your plate with veggies and fruit, a quarter with protein, a quarter with carbs, and add a little healthy fat. That's a balanced meal — every single time.

Good to know

Common questions

Is this medical or personalized nutrition advice?

No — everything here is general educational content to help you build good habits. For individualized medical nutrition therapy, work with a registered dietitian one-on-one.

Do I have to count calories or macros?

Not at all. The balanced-plate method is designed to work without tracking. If you ever want to dial things in further, tracking is just one optional tool — not a requirement.

Can I eat the foods I love?

Yes. No food is off-limits. It's about your overall pattern, not any single meal — balance and consistency beat restriction every time.

Are the guides really free?

Yep. The guides are free to download. Down the road there may be optional premium resources, but the fundamentals will always be free.
